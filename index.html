REALITY CHECK

THE "CLOUD" IS A LIE.

01

Owned by Giants

Over 95% of the world's internet capacity is carried by cables owned or co-owned by just five companies: Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, and a handful of telecoms. The internet is not neutral infrastructure—it is privately held.

02

Geopolitically Volatile

In 2022, cables were severed near Tonga, isolating 100,000 people for five weeks. In wartime, submarines could sever internet between continents. NATO has identified submarine cable attacks as a critical national security threat.

03

Physically Fragile

Near shorelines, cables are buried under the seabed but in deep water they simply rest on the ocean floor—as thin as a garden hose. A dragging ship anchor in the wrong place can silence a region's internet instantly.
